# Startup Launch Case Study

## Scenario

A SaaS startup preparing for MVP launch experienced multiple operational risks during the final release week.

The Technical Project Manager coordinated Agile delivery, QA stabilisation, release readiness, and stakeholder communication to support successful launch execution.

---

# Product

AI-powered SaaS CRM platform for startup sales teams.

---

# Key Challenges

## QA Instability
Regression testing identified authentication session failures during final staging validation.

### Impact
- delayed UAT approval
- increased release risk
- stakeholder concern

---

## Scope Creep
Founders requested additional dashboard features during final sprint.

### Impact
- sprint overload risk
- potential launch delay
- engineering bandwidth strain

---

## Third-Party Integration Delays
The email delivery provider experienced intermittent API instability.

### Impact
- password reset failures
- onboarding delays
- operational risk increase

---

# TPM Actions Taken

## Risk Coordination
- prioritised critical authentication stability
- escalated API failures immediately
- enforced sprint scope controls

---

## QA Stabilization
- coordinated regression retesting
- aligned QA and engineering workflows
- tracked critical bug resolution

---

## Stakeholder Communication
- provided daily status updates
- communicated release risks clearly
- aligned launch expectations with leadership

---

## Release Readiness
- validated rollback procedures
- coordinated final QA signoff
- monitored production deployment

---

# Outcome

## Successful MVP Launch
Core authentication workflows were released successfully with no critical production outages.

---

## Operational Improvements
- improved QA coordination process
- reduced sprint scope instability
- enhanced release communication workflows
